Aglio E Olio

Home

Description

"Aglio e olio" is a classic Italian pasta dish that translates to "garlic and oil," meaning it's essentially spaghetti tossed in a simple sauce made with just garlic, olive oil, and sometimes a pinch of red pepper flakes, highlighting the pure flavors of these few ingredients; considered a staple of Neapolitan cuisine, it's known for its simplicity and quick preparation time.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound uncooked spaghetti
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 6 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • salt and pepper to tase
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 cup finely grate Parmigiano-reggiano cheese

Steps

  1. Gather Ingredients
  2. Boil water, cook spaghetti in water roughly 10-12 minutes
  3. While pasta is cooking, combine olive oil and grlic in a cold skillet
  4. Cook over medium heat to toast garlic about 10 minutes. Reduce heat to medium-low when olive oil begins to bubble. Cook and stir until garlic is golden brown. Remove from heat
  5. Stir red pepper flakes, salt and pepper into pasta
  6. Pour in hot olive oil and garlic, sprinkle parsley and cheese into pasta. Toss until comibned
  7. Serve!
